# Hooks & UGC Scripting Reference

Companion to `roi-to-engagement.md`. Use this BEFORE generating a reel / ad, to pick the right hook, structure the arc, and validate line-by-line. Then use TRIBE v2 to empirically verify the choices on the rendered video.

---

## 1. Five Awareness Stages

Hook strategy changes completely by stage. Misidentify the stage → hook falls flat regardless of cleverness.

| Stage | Viewer knows | Hook must | Must NOT |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Unaware** | Nothing about the problem | Surface a hidden pain or opportunity | Mention product |
| **Problem-Aware** | Has the pain, doesn't know solutions exist | Name the pain precisely | Sell yet |
| **Solution-Aware** | Knows category exists | Show category differentiation | Re-educate |
| **Product-Aware** | Knows your product | Show specific benefit or social proof | Re-pitch fundamentals |
| **Most-Aware** | Ready to buy, waiting for reason | Urgency / scarcity / offer | Soft-pitch |

**Rule of thumb:** *Remarketing audiences = Product-Aware. Cold reels = Unaware or Problem-Aware.*

For VIA:
- Cold reels → **Problem-Aware** ("you're building blind")
- Retargeting → **Product-Aware** ("247 signals since you last logged in")
- Never pitch VIA to the Unaware cold — too abstract.

## 2. Eight Psychological Triggers

Every hook should lead with ONE of these. Layering two is stronger.

| Trigger | Mechanism | VIA example |
|---|---|---|
| **Pattern Interrupt** | Break expected grammar / framing | "Your CRM is a graveyard." |
| **Identity Call-Out** | Name the viewer's role | "If you're a founder under €1M ARR..." |
| **Pain Agitation** | Press on a known ache | "Three years building. Blind." |
| **Curiosity Gap** | Ask a question only the video answers | "Who was gonna tell me there are 247 opportunities right now?" |
| **Social Proof** | Cite outcomes from peers | "Three founders I know already moved on this signal." |
| **Contrarian** | Say the opposite of the trope | "Automation doesn't grow your business. Attention does." |
| **Aspiration** | Future-state their identity | "Founders who see signals first win the next round." |
| **Urgency** | Time-bound window | "Nine days on this grant. Then it's gone." |

## 3. Video Hook — Triple-Layer Rule

For every video hook (first 3–5s), write THREE layers simultaneously:

1. **Spoken** — voice-over or on-camera line.
2. **Visual** — what's literally on screen. Must be interesting in isolation (muted autoplay).
3. **Text Overlay** — caption / kinetic type. Half the audience watches muted.

**Failure mode:** great spoken hook + boring visual = scroll. Great visual + generic overlay = no comprehension on mute.

VIA example hook:
- **Spoken:** "Who was gonna tell me there are 247 opportunities in my market — right now."
- **Visual:** founder's POV, screen floods with pulsing dots, counter slams 0→247.
- **Overlay:** `247 · RIGHT NOW`.

All three must be legible independently. Then they amplify each other.

## 5. UGC Emotional Arc (for anything 15–90s)

```
HOOK  →  PROBLEM  →  SOLUTION  →  PROOF  →  CTA
 3s        7-12s       10-15s     8-12s    3-5s
```

Add **CONTRARIAN** after PROOF if legally sensitive (see VIA below).

Each beat has a job:
- **Hook:** stop the scroll. No exposition.
- **Problem:** viewer recognizes themselves. Emotion before logic.
- **Solution:** name it once. Don't explain mechanics.
- **Proof:** show, don't tell. Numbers, artifacts, faces.
- **Contrarian:** flip the obvious claim. Builds trust AND handles legal positioning.
- **CTA:** one verb. Time-bound.

## 6. Read-Aloud Test

Before recording: read every line out loud.

- Would a real person say this in a bar? → keep.
- Sounds like a deck bullet? → rewrite.
- Have to pause to breathe mid-sentence? → break into two.
- Ends in a noun? → usually dead. End in a verb or a punch.

**VIA lines that pass:** "Signals don't wait." "You notice. You decide." "Three years building. Blind."

**VIA lines that fail:** "VIA is the intelligent platform that empowers founders to discover opportunities." (deck bullet, no one says this).

## 7. Word Count Targets by Length

| Duration | VO words | Scene count | Lines on screen |
|---|---|---|---|
| 15–30s | 40–75 | 3–4 | ≤6 |
| 30–60s | 75–150 | 5–6 | ≤10 |
| 60–90s | 150–225 | 6–8 | ≤14 |

Under-count is fine (silence works). Over-count = rushed delivery = low DAN (attention) in TRIBE results.

## 8. Voice Pattern Swipe File

Proven opening patterns. Swap the nouns, keep the rhythm.

- "Who was gonna tell me ___?"
- "My hot take is ___."
- "I have been gatekeeping ___."
- "If you're a [role] and you still [behavior], stop."
- "There's one thing ___ and nobody talks about it."
- "I spent [time] doing [thing]. Here's what I missed."
- "This is the [thing] that cost me [outcome]."
- "You are doing [thing] wrong. Here's why."
- "If I had to start [again] tomorrow, I'd ___."
- "POV: you're a [role] and you just realized ___."
- "Nobody is ready for this conversation about ___."
- "Three [things]. One of them is a lie."
- "[Number] reasons [obvious belief] is wrong."
- "I stopped [doing X]. I started [doing Y]. Everything changed."

## 9. VIA-Specific Overlay Rules

VIA is a legally-sensitive product (EU AI Act, hallucination liability). Copy compliance:

**Green phrases:**
- "VIA surfaces ___."
- "VIA shows what's moving."
- "You notice. You decide."
- "Signals you would have missed."
- "Intelligence, not answers."

**Red phrases — never ship:**
- "VIA tells you what to do."
- "VIA decides."
- "VIA recommends this action." (strong "recommend" — weak OK)
- "Let VIA drive."
- "AI makes the call."

Every VIA hook must route the agency through the viewer, not through VIA. The **CONTRARIAN scene** in a reel is where this is made explicit: *"VIA doesn't tell me what to do. It shows me what's moving. I decide."*

## 10. Closing the Loop with TRIBE v2

After writing, recording, and editing a reel using this reference:

1. Render to `.mp4`.
2. Upload to Colab (see `colab-snippet.md`), download `preds.npy`.
3. Run `analyze.py --preds preds.npy`.
4. Check the report:
   - **Hook valley?** Your first 3s isn't stopping scroll. Go back to §2/§3.
   - **Problem peak but Solution valley?** Solution beat is abstract — concretize with a visible artifact.
   - **Proof valley + DMN rise?** Cards or screenshots are boring — tighten to 3 max, pulse one.
   - **CONTRARIAN peak?** Legal-safe positioning is also the strongest moment emotionally. Good.
   - **CTA valley?** Either too soft (no verb / no urgency) or too aggressive (limbic suppressed). Read-Aloud test again.

TRIBE tells you which beat failed. This reference tells you how to fix it.
